# 02 · Environment Design

The museum is one corridor of intent: a visitor should always know where to look,
where to go next, and never feel lost or backtrack. Each space is a complete shell
generated by `Tooling/Blender/gen_architecture.py` (floor slab, four walls with a
framed doorway, ceiling, baseboard and cornice), positioned to line up with the
Unity room roots. Rooms are **activated independently** at runtime, so they need
not physically touch in world space — `SceneController` repositions the player rig
to each room's `playerAnchor` on entry.

## Flow and pacing

```
Splash ──► Atrium ──► Ancient ──► WWII ──► Vault ──► Reveal ──► (Complete)
 PLAY      orient     Caesar       Enigma   passphrase  synthesis
           + tutorial  disk         machine   vault       sculpture
```

Target full walkthrough: **~2:30–2:50**. Each exhibit is sized so a first-time
visitor reads the plaque, experiments, solves, and is rewarded within ~30–40 s.
Gating guarantees the order; there is no way to skip ahead.

## Room-by-room

### Splash
A dark, quiet antechamber with a single glowing **PLAY** button and a few
non-verbal **tutorial cards** that mime the core interactions (grab, poke, point).
Purpose: let the headset settle, teach the verbs, set a hushed "archive" tone.
Lighting: low, one warm key on the button. Ambient: `amb_atrium` (soft).

### Atrium
The orientation hub. A calm, lofty space that establishes the museum's visual
language (warm plaster, dark trim, brass accents) and points clearly toward the
first exhibit. A title treatment and a one-line "what you'll do here" plaque.
Purpose: tone + wayfinding, no puzzle. Ambient: `amb_atrium`.

### Ancient Cryptography (Caesar)
Centrepiece: the **Caesar cipher disk** on a stone pedestal at comfortable
standing height. Outer ring shows the fixed ciphertext alphabet; the inner ring is
grabbed and twisted. Two plaques: one frames Caesar historically, one shows the
encoded message. As the visitor rotates, mapping lines are drawn between letter
pairs and the decoded readout updates live; solving at **+3** reveals
`CROSS THE RUBICON` and chimes. Lighting: warm, slightly torch-lit, focused on the
disk. Ambient: `amb_ancient` (airy stone room + faint wind).

### The Enigma Era (WWII)
Centrepiece: the **Enigma-inspired machine** — three lettered rotors, a historical
keyboard, a lampboard, a plugboard accent and a commit lever. The visitor sets the
rotors to **MAC**, types `ZLDFDQO`, and watches the decoded word build to
**`VICTORY`** while a signal trace renders the current path through the rotors to
the lit lamp. Pulling the lever commits the solution (power-up + chime). Plaques
cover the operational drama and what makes Enigma poly-alphabetic. Lighting:
cooler, instrument-panel feel, with the lamp glow as the hero light. Ambient:
`amb_wwii` (mains hum + faint teleprinter ticks).

### Modern Security (Vault)
Centrepiece: a **heavy vault door** with a circular locking ring, a laser-pointer
**keypad** (A–Z + Enter/Clear) and red status lights. The visitor types the word
recovered from the Enigma — **`VICTORY`** — and on a correct entry the status flips
green, the ring spins, the door opens, the room warms, and an emissive archive is
revealed. Plaque ties the millennia-old principle to modern encryption ("the
secret is only useful to whoever holds the key"). Lighting: clean and modern,
cool key + the cyan archive glow as reveal. Ambient: `amb_vault` (server-room hum).

### The Reveal
A focused chamber with a single sculpture on a plinth and one plaque. On entry the
sculpture **morphs through the three eras** — Roman stele → clockwork gears →
circuit lattice — as the conclusion text fades up and a final chord resolves, then
the experience reaches `Complete`. Lighting: theatrical, a single warm-to-cool
sweep following the morph. Ambient: `amb_atrium` (returns to calm).

## Wayfinding principles

- **One focal point per room.** The hero object is always the brightest, most
  central, most contrasty thing. Lighting does the pointing.
- **Doorways frame the next beat.** The exit doorway is visible from the exhibit so
  "where next" is never a question; it only opens once the exhibit is solved.
- **Plaques are short and positioned at eye height** beside (not on) the exhibit, so
  reading never blocks interaction.
- **Colour temperature tells the era**: warm torchlight (ancient) → neutral
  instrument light (WWII) → cool clean light (modern) → theatrical (reveal).

## Lighting strategy (summary; perf detail in `05`)

All lighting is **baked**. Each room has a small set of baked lights and a
reflection probe (referenced from its `RoomDescriptor`). Because only one room is
active at a time, per-room lightmaps stay small and load cost is trivial. Emissive
materials (lamp, status lights, archive glow, hologram) provide the dynamic-looking
accents without runtime light cost; the few values that change at runtime are
driven via `MaterialPropertyBlock` (no material instancing, no GI rebuild).
